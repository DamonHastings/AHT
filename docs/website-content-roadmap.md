# Website content roadmap

Distilled from a content strategy conversation. Use this as the north star for copy, structure, and design updates.

---

## 1. Brand essence (from questionnaire)

**Tone and feeling (not literal copy on every page):** honest, spirited, youthful, movement, power, fulminant, challenge, breath, bliss, time, authenticity, grounded, loving.

**Visual direction:** type of play, color, movement; colors that echo the office; imagery that matches the work—not every keyword on the page, but the *essence* of the practice.

**Primary action:** make scheduling a consultation the clearest next step (aligned with how people actually reach out: Psychology Today → email/voicemail asking for a consultation).

---

## 2. Positioning and fit

- **Be explicit about the specialty** (expressive arts, movement, creativity, challenge). That self-selects clients who want it; it is OK not to be for everyone.
- **Distinction:** attract people who *want* this work or are open/motivated; it is fine if movement or creative approaches are *hard* for someone—that is different from not wanting them.
- **Warmth:** consultations should feel welcoming; celebrate the courage of taking the first step (convey this in hero/supporting copy, not necessarily as a “tagline”).
- **How people find you (signals to reflect):** relatable presence (photo/personality), teens and grief, creative/movement angle (some drawn to it, some to other facets). Copy should not assume everyone loves movement—it should clarify openness vs. opposition.

---

## 3. Hero and above-the-fold

| Element | Direction |
|--------|-----------|
| **Photo** | Practitioner in the actual therapy space, in the chair, authentic setting. Temporary use of existing photo is acceptable until a new shoot (e.g., after haircut). |
| **Intro** | One short, welcoming line (iterate versions)—not forced “marketing speak”; should feel genuine. |
| **Supporting** | One to two sentences that state specialty clearly (e.g., expressive arts therapy) and give a plain-language overview of *how* you practice. |

**Practice overview (themes to weave in, paraphrase for site):**

- Warm, accepting, curious, playful, humorous; also willing to bring loving challenge and tolerable discomfort (“like home, but gently prodded”).
- Modalities flex to the person: metaphor, symbolism, movement, color, imagery, nature when it fits.
- **Collaborative and directive:** responsive to what they bring; co-created direction; avoids the “only the therapist talks” deadness.
- Pull language from **Psychology Today** profile where it already matches.

---

## 4. Site structure: audience sections → dedicated pages

**Approach:** homepage (or hub) with clear segments; each segment links to a **fuller dedicated page** (“article length” is acceptable—short blurbs on the hub, depth on child pages).

**Segments (all validated—not “too many” if each is real work):**

1. Kids  
2. Teens  
3. Young adults  
4. Parents / single or co-parents  
5. Grief and life transitions  
6. ADHD (and related neurodivergence—see nuance below)  
7. SEED / UC Davis–adjacent student support (in-town, family system, downtown proximity)  
8. Groups (future—see §7)  
9. **Anxiety** (cross-cutting—see §6)

**Neurodivergence framing:** prefer language that helps ADHD/autistic clients self-identify and find you without a generic “I work with everyone on the spectrum” billboard; let the right people recognize themselves.

---

## 5. Segment notes (copy seeds)

### Kids

- Focus: communication, using voice, self-confidence/esteem, friendships, how they show up at home, fears/odd associations with things, playful work parents may not replicate.
- **Not a focus:** heavy “anger / emotional regulation” as a headline if it implies highly explosive kids **in public settings** (explosive at home may be OK; explosive in other settings—not ideal fit).
- **Parent engagement:** ages ~7–12 → monthly parent check-in; 13–15 → roughly every couple of months, adjusted by need; 16–18 → roughly every ~3 months or as needed by acuity, length of treatment, crisis risk.

### Teens

- Davis-area transitions (e.g., 9th–10th), juniors/seniors, transition to college.
- Friendships, relationships, behavior choices, prefrontal cortex / insight in age-appropriate ways.
- Real talk about choices and future (college, etc.) without harshness—adult voice that is not parent, not day-to-day caregiver; playful, relational, connected to their interests.

### Young adults

- Independence skills: scheduling, telling parents when struggling, knowing resources.
- Be an **anchor** so the world feels less scary (short version on hub; expand on page).

### Parents / single or co-parents

- Emotional support for co-parent loneliness (wins they cannot share with the other parent), behavior/discipline, parenting stress—normalize, validate, share experience when appropriate, practical guidance (assessments, “did you tell the dentist…”, etc.).
- Parenting while **wounded by own parents**, absent/lacking models, or parenting after loss—explicitly welcome this thread.

### Grief

- Life transitions, identity shifts, pets, people, ambiguous loss, regret (“chose one job over another”), nebulous unnamed grief—stuck, angry, sad, confused. Educate lightly: grief is broader than obvious bereavement.

### ADHD

- **Relational impact** of growing up and moving through the world with ADHD (or uncertainty before diagnosis)—self-esteem, relationships, feeling misunderstood vs. “here is how we fix you” only.
- Practical support when wanted: CBT-ish tools, lists, body doubling, calls together, etc.
- Assessment support without pushing medication; for those on meds: whole-person check-ins (eating, mood, blunting, emotional baseline, “still feel like yourself”)—psychiatrist/psychologist cadence vs. weekly therapy.
- Marsico (or similar) assessment resource mentioned as useful for clarity.

### SEED students (and similar)

- In-town tie to family/caregivers; frequency of contact as needed.
- Social navigation (wanting connection with general student population, roommate issues), institutional rules (freshman restrictions, not alone on campus, etc.), tension between “college freedom” and program structure.
- Immature vs. mature aspects of cognition—support without judgment.

### Anxiety (cross-cutting)

- Stress, worry, identifying activation moments, reducing symptoms, communicating needs to family/support.
- Normalize intrusive/weird thoughts (including harm-related intrusive thoughts); CBT where comfortable; **relational** container (“I’m here with you; I don’t live with you” so less overload than home).
- Optional angle: anxiety as **activating energy** that can be harnessed (contrast with depression as heavier—position anxiety work as a strength of the practice without dismissing depression seekers if you still take them).

---

## 6. Groups (phase 2)

- **Concept:** adults who have lost parents; and/or a group about **latching onto hope and positivity** under bombardment—to support living, striving, connecting (rebellious act of positivity framing).
- **Launch:** defer formal push until demand or co-facilitator exists; optional “interest list” or soft mention so inbound interest drives legwork.
- **Content:** when live, consider short suggested readings (e.g., why group therapy works)—same pattern as other “learn more” resources.

---

## 7. Resources and credibility (later / ongoing)

- **Suggested readings** per topic or audience (articles behind paywalls acceptable over time as practice invests).
- **For clinicians:** shareable knowledge, referral-friendly depth (peer referral example given).
- Research-forward voice is a **goal identity**—implement gradually; do not block launch.

---

## 8. Design and UX implications

- **Feeling** from color, typography, imagery, motion—not from stuffing the questionnaire onto the page.
- **One clear primary CTA:** schedule consultation (mirror Psychology Today → email/voicemail path).
- **Scannable hub + deep pages** to avoid overwhelming a single scroll while still honoring real specialties.

---

## 9. Content sourcing and ownership

- **Psychology Today** is a canonical source for overlapping copy.
- **Questionnaire** (words, images, play, color, movement) informs visual and tonal direction.
- **Michelle** provided direction and examples; final site copy can be drafted/edited collaboratively—no need to guess “who wrote” placeholder text on staging.

---

## 10. Implementation checklist (prioritized)

1. Lock hero: photo plan, welcome line variants, 2-sentence practice overview + CTA.  
2. Homepage: segment grid/cards with short blurbs → internal routes.  
3. Build **dedicated pages** for each segment using the notes in §5 (article-length OK).  
4. Add **anxiety** as explicit cross-cutting theme (hub blurb + optional dedicated or shared page).  
5. Tune **ADHD/neurodivergence** language for self-selection vs. clinical billboard.  
6. **SEED** page: local, family-bridge, social/rules, developmental nuance.  
7. **Grief** page: broaden definition + examples (regret, ambiguous loss).  
8. **Parents** page: co-parent, discipline, stress, wounded-parent lineage, after-loss parenting.  
9. Reserve **groups** section or footer note for future; interest capture if desired.  
10. Backlog: suggested readings, clinician-facing resources, paid article access as budget allows.  
11. Replace hero photo when new session in-office imagery is ready.
